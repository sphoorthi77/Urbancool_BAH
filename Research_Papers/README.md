## UrbanCool
### Optimizing Urban Heat Mitigation and Cooling Strategies using Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning

---

## About

This folder contains the research papers, literature review, and scientific references that formed the foundation of the UrbanCool project.

Our solution combines:

- Artificial Intelligence
- Machine Learning
- Physics-Informed AI
- Remote Sensing
- GIS
- Urban Climate Modelling
- Heat Stress Analysis
- Scenario-Based Cooling Optimization

using satellite imagery, meteorological datasets, and urban morphology to generate optimized cooling strategies for cities.

## Paper 2

Title:
Enhancing Urban Heat Island Analysis Through Multisensor Data Fusion and GRU-Based Deep Learning Approaches for Climate Modeling

Publisher:
IEEE JSTARS, Vol. 18, 2025

Official Link:
https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/stamp/stamp.jsp?arnumber=10938603

Contribution to UrbanCool:

- GRU-based recurrent deep learning model for UHI/SUHI forecasting using NDVI and NDBI as predictors
- 23-year (2001–2023) monthly Landsat time series workflow built entirely in Google Earth Engine
- Modified Mann–Kendall trend test and Sen's Slope for detecting seasonal/annual UHI, NDVI, NDBI trends
- Continuous and discrete wavelet transform (Morlet, coif1) for time-frequency correlation between UHI and vegetation/built-up indices
- SHAP-based feature importance to rank environmental drivers of UHI
- Benchmark comparison of GRU vs. LSTM architectures for thermal prediction accuracy

## Paper 7

Title:
Spatiotemporal Assessment of Urban Thermal Discomfort in Kolkata, India: Insights From Cloud-Based Remote Sensing

Publisher:
IEEE JSTARS, Vol. 18, 2025

Official Link:
https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/stamp/stamp.jsp?arnumber=11154444

Contribution to UrbanCool:

- 30-year (1995–2024) cloud-based GEE workflow fusing Landsat 5/7/8 data for long-term thermal monitoring
- Urban Thermal Field Variance Index (UTFVI) as an ecological + human-comfort thermal stress metric, beyond standard UHI
- Five-tier ecological stress classification (No/Slight/Moderate/High/Extreme Stress) for actionable urban heat zoning
- Sen's Slope Estimator and modified Mann-Kendall trend tests for statistically robust spatiotemporal trend mapping
- NDVI–LST–UHI–UTFVI integration showing vegetation loss as a key driver of escalating thermal discomfort
- Policy-oriented framework linking remote sensing outputs to green infrastructure and cool-roof interventions

## Paper 9

Title:
Quantitative Analysis of Urban Heat Island and ISA Density Effects on Land Surface Temperature: A Remote Sensing Study Using Local Morphological Density Analysis and Hybrid Optimization Model

Publisher:
IEEE JSTARS, Vol. 19, 2026

Official Link:
https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/stamp/stamp.jsp?arnumber=11260902

Contribution to UrbanCool:

- Novel hybrid Genetic Algorithm–Simulated Annealing + Generalized Additive Model (GA-SA/GAM) for adaptive, data-driven UHI threshold detection (replacing static mean+0.5σ methods)
- Local Morphological Density (LMD) / granulometric analysis to generate continuous ISA and green-space density maps
- ASTER & Landsat 7 LST retrieval and cross-sensor validation pipeline (RMSE, MAE, Bias against NASA POWER EST data)
- Quantified nonlinear ISA-density vs. LST relationship with explicit critical thresholds (~20–30% ISA) for two Mediterranean cities (Algiers, Taranto)
- Demonstrated green-space density's cooling effect on LST, reinforcing UrbanCool's green-infrastructure optimization goals
- Transferable computational framework combining optimization + statistical modeling for UHI zone delineation
